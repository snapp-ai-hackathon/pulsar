Pulsar – Smart Driver Distribution Prototype

A compressed walkthrough of how Pulsar ingests surge signals, applies time-series forecasting, and delivers measurable rider impact. Built for the Snapp AI Hackathon, this slide deck condenses our architecture, impact metrics, and roadmap into a single, animated narrative.

01 · Solution Stack

Data & Signals

CSV snapshots, ClickHouse exports, and optional Redis/Rabbit collectors feed SnapshotBuilder + TimeSeriesStore. We can stay productive even without direct prod access.

Forecasting

Hybrid Prophet + LSTM/Temporal Fusion per (city, service type, H3) with sliding-window retraining and operator overrides. ElasticNet keeps predictions interpretable for ops.

Decision Engine

Multi-objective ranking with fairness constraints, driver fuel/time estimates, and delta-earning KPIs, feeding Pulsar API & dashboards backed by MLflow.

02 · Time-Series Algorithms

Prophet Baseline

  • Captures daily/weekly seasonality per service/city.
  • Low-latency fallback for new hexagons with sparse history.

Sequence Models

  • LSTM / Temporal-Fusion networks leverage krings (adjacent hex influence) and capture surge ripple effects.
  • Lagged features (acceptance, supply, price-conversion) created per parquet history.

Online ElasticNet

  • Fast retraining hook via `train-online` triggered from NATS.
  • ElasticNet tuned for interpretability; upper bounds saturate extreme surge values.

03 · Surge Prediction Engine

Predict the Spike

Temporal-Fusion + Prophet track krings and rider demand to raise a “surge window” flag up to 30 minutes before pricing reacts. Ops sees the spike before it punches them in the face.

Pre-position Supply

ElasticNet scores hexagons with travel cost + fatigue so we only ping the right crowd. Drivers start moving while riders are still deciding whether to open the app.

Explain Every Alert

MLflow + TimeSeriesStore let us replay the exact features that triggered a surge prediction. When ops asks “why did you ping district 2?”, we have receipts.

In short, Pulsar doesn’t just react to surge — it narrates the surge before it happens, giving drivers, riders, and ops a head start.

04 · Why It Matters

Even as a prototype, Pulsar couples CSV backfill, TimeSeriesStore, and NATS-triggered retraining to unlock tangible wins for Snapp’s ecosystem. We keep the claims qualitative because this build hasn’t hit production yet, but the direction is clear:

Happier Riders

  • Smoother surge curves mean fewer shocks when opening the app.
  • Shorter wait windows as drivers get nudged before demand spikes.

Calmer Drivers

  • Recommendations factor travel time + fatigue, not just surge.
  • Explainable ElasticNet helps ops justify every push notification.

Ops Visibility

  • Parquet history + MLflow runs give instant “what changed?” answers.
  • NATS retrain button: try, observe, revert — all in minutes.

Bottom line: faster forecasts + fairer decisions reduce customer churn, increase driver loyalty, and let ops teams experiment without fear.

06 · How the Pieces Flow

1 · Signals In

ClickHouse exporter pushes fresh rows into NATS/JetStream, while CSV backfills keep prototypes alive when prod access is limited. Legacy Redis/Rabbit connectors exist but are purely optional now.

2 · Intelligence

ElasticNet, Prophet, and Temporal-Fusion models roll over parquet histories; MLflow tracks every run.

3 · Decisions Out

Pulsar API ranks hexagons, surfaces driver tips, and streams a live dashboard for ops + referees.

4 · Instant Retrain

A NATS event (`train-online`) rebuilds snapshots and re-trains models in minutes. No Rabbit, no Redis — just publish to NATS and let Pulsar refresh the ElasticNet/TFN stack automatically.
